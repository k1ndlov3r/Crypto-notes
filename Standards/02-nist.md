# National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)

## Overview

The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) is a U.S. government agency responsible for developing standards, guidelines, and best practices across many technical fields, including cybersecurity and cryptography.

NIST publishes security frameworks, cryptographic recommendations, and technical guidance used worldwide.

---

## Responsibilities

- Develop cybersecurity standards
- Publish cryptographic recommendations
- Evaluate cryptographic algorithms
- Conduct security research
- Support federal agencies

---

## Important Publications

### NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

Provides guidance for managing cybersecurity risks using five core functions:

- Identify
- Protect
- Detect
- Respond
- Recover

---

### Special Publications (SP 800 Series)

The SP 800 series contains detailed cybersecurity guidance.

Examples include:

- SP 800-53 — Security and Privacy Controls
- SP 800-61 — Incident Handling Guide
- SP 800-63 — Digital Identity Guidelines
- SP 800-171 — Protecting Controlled Unclassified Information

---

### Cryptographic Standards

NIST also standardizes:

- AES
- SHA-2
- SHA-3
- Random Number Generators
- Post-Quantum Cryptography algorithms

---

## Why NIST Matters

NIST publications are widely adopted by:

- Governments
- Enterprises
- Universities
- Security professionals

Even organizations outside the United States frequently use NIST guidance as a security baseline.

---

## Summary

NIST is one of the world's most influential cybersecurity organizations. Its standards and publications form the foundation for modern information security programs.
